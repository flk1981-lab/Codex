# Research Gap Backlog

Purpose:

- Maintain a ranked backlog of unresolved, decision-relevant research opportunities.

For each gap:

- question
- why it matters clinically
- what evidence already exists
- why the gap remains
- recommended design
- feasibility notes
- priority: high / medium / low

- question: Which TB diagnostic or screening strategies remain decision-superior once sample type, care setting, turnaround time, and downstream management consequences are evaluated together?
- why it matters clinically: Programs need evidence that improves real care pathways, not only analytic performance under ideal conditions.
- what evidence already exists: WHO diagnostics activity on 2026-03-24 and the 2026-03-17 screening data call indicate an active policy evidence appetite, but recent daily surveillance did not surface a new clinically decisive primary study.
- why the gap remains: Many candidate studies emphasize isolated accuracy or efficacy signals without showing transportable management impact across decentralized settings.
- recommended design: Pragmatic prospective diagnostic or screening evaluation with pre-specified policy-relevant endpoints and explicit downstream management capture.
- feasibility notes: Can be structured as a staged evidence package across centralized and decentralized sites rather than a single efficacy-only dataset.
- priority: high
