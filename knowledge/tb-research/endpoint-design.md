# Endpoint Design

Purpose:

- Build a reusable endpoint library for TB and adjacent clinical research.

Capture:

- microbiological endpoints
- clinical endpoints
- radiological endpoints
- patient-centered endpoints
- composite endpoint cautions
- timing windows
- relapse and recurrence logic
- treatment completion vs durable cure distinctions

For each entry:

- endpoint candidate
- best use case
- main advantage
- main failure mode
- bias or interpretability risk

- endpoint candidate: policy-aligned diagnostic primary endpoint linked to downstream management
- best use case: TB diagnostic or screening studies intended to inform program or guideline decisions
- main advantage: captures whether sample type, setting, turnaround time, and treatment-action consequences produce clinically usable evidence rather than a technically isolated accuracy signal
- main failure mode: relying on surrogate or laboratory-only endpoints that look strong analytically but do not change care pathways
- bias or interpretability risk: endpoint may overstate value if management consequences are undefined or if follow-on care access differs across settings
