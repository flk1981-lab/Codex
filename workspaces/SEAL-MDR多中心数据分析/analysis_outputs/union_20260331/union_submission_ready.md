**Title**

Interim multicenter outcomes of four all-oral shortened regimens in the SEAL-MDR study for MDR-TB in China

Word count: 16

**Summary**

Building on MDR-Chin, SEAL-MDR further explores whether all-oral MDR-TB regimens can be delivered with shorter duration. This interim multicenter analysis describes arm-specific outcomes for four protocol-defined 6-9 month regimens in China.

Word count: 31

**Abstract Body**

**Background:** SEAL-MDR was designed to build on the MDR-Chin study and further explore whether effective all-oral MDR-TB regimens could be delivered with even shorter treatment duration. We report interim multicenter outcomes from the ongoing SEAL-MDR study in China.

**Methods:** Using the existing curated Excel dataset only, we removed exact duplicate rows and analyzed protocol-defined arms A-D. Regimens were A, Bdq/Lzd/Mfx/Cs; B, Lzd/Mfx/Cs/Cfz/Z; C, Bdq/Lzd/Cs/Cfz/Z; and D, Lzd/Cs/Cfz/Z plus sulfasalazine, each delivered for 6-9 months according to protocol response. Month-2 and end-of-treatment (EOT) culture negativity were calculated among evaluable records with explicit positive or negative results. Post-treatment unfavorable outcomes were estimated among records with mature follow-up and assessable status.

**Results:** After exact de-duplication, 601 analyzable records from 25 centers were available across arms A-D. Month-2 culture negativity was 294/317, 92.7% in arm A; 82/96, 85.4% in arm B; 62/74, 83.8% in arm C; and 32/42, 76.2% in arm D. EOT culture negativity was 293/302, 97.0% in arm A; 68/72, 94.4% in arm B; 66/69, 95.7% in arm C; and 25/29, 86.2% in arm D. Twelve-month unfavorable outcomes among assessable records were 47/251, 18.7% in arm A; 9/50, 18.0% in arm B; 5/58, 8.6% in arm C; and 4/6, 66.7% in arm D.

**Conclusions:** In this interim multicenter analysis, arms A-C showed high early culture negativity and high EOT culture negativity, with the lowest 12-month unfavorable outcome rate in arm C. Arm D had the least mature follow-up and should be interpreted cautiously. Continued follow-up completion will strengthen final comparative analyses.
