## Preferred Category

## Preferred Title

Confirmed post-treatment outcomes of a pretomanid-free bedaquiline-delamanid-linezolid strategy with or without moxifloxacin for rifampicin-resistant tuberculosis in China

## Alternate Short Title

Post-treatment outcomes of a pretomanid-free BDL/M strategy for RR-TB in China

## Recommended Abstract

### Background

WHO-endorsed short-course regimens for rifampicin-resistant tuberculosis (RR-TB) include pretomanid, but pretomanid remains unavailable in some settings. We evaluated post-treatment outcomes of a pretomanid-free strategy using bedaquiline, delamanid and linezolid, with or without moxifloxacin (BDL/M), in an ongoing strategy-guided clinical study in China.

### Methods

We performed an interim descriptive analysis of protocol-consistent participants treated with BDL or BDLM. To distinguish biological treatment failure from follow-up attrition, we reconstructed strict post-treatment endpoints separating confirmed failure/relapse from loss to follow-up and withdrawal. The primary endpoint for this analysis was confirmed failure/relapse by 6 months after treatment completion; the secondary endpoint was confirmed failure/relapse by 12 months. Month-2 culture conversion and end-of-treatment culture status were also summarized.

### Results

Among 122 entered records, 121 unique participants remained after deduplication; 108 protocol-consistent participants were included (49 BDL, 59 BDLM). Among evaluable participants, month-2 culture conversion was 90/101 (89.1%; 95% CI 81.3-94.4), and end-of-treatment culture negativity was 78/78 (100.0%; 95% CI 95.4-100.0). Confirmed failure/relapse occurred in 2/108 participants by 6 months post-treatment (1.9%; 95% CI 0.2-6.5) and remained 2/108 by 12 months (1.9%; 95% CI 0.2-6.5). At 6 months, 17/108 (15.7%) were lost to follow-up and 8/108 (7.4%) had withdrawn; at 12 months, 36/108 (33.3%) were lost to follow-up and 8/108 (7.4%) had withdrawn. Both confirmed failure/relapse events occurred in the BDL group; none were observed in the BDLM group, although treatment allocation was strategy-guided rather than randomized.

### Conclusions

In this interim analysis from a pretomanid-unavailable setting, a pretomanid-free BDL/M strategy showed strong microbiological response and a low rate of confirmed post-treatment failure/relapse. Apparent long-term unfavorable outcomes were driven mainly by follow-up attrition rather than confirmed biological recurrence. Participants classified as lost to follow-up at 12 months remain under active tracing.

- If the system has a strict word limit, shorten the Methods sentence before cutting the Results sentence on confirmed failure/relapse.
